# International Content Architecture

## Goal
Build a multilingual content system for international transport of the deceased to Poland that:
- matches real user intent,
- supports urgent conversion,
- scales across countries and cities without thin content,
- stays useful under stress.

## Core Page Types

### 1. Service Pages
Purpose:
- capture broad commercial intent,
- explain the service model,
- route users into country and route pages.

Mandatory value:
- clear process,
- document overview,
- transport mode logic,
- strong trust and urgent CTA.

### 2. Country Pages
Purpose:
- explain how transport from a given country usually works,
- reflect country-specific documents and logistics,
- open the path to city pages and route pages.

Mandatory value:
- country-specific paperwork,
- institution handling,
- transport mode guidance,
- city cluster links.

### 3. City Pages
Purpose:
- confirm pickup and handling from a specific city,
- answer local uncertainty,
- route users toward exact city -> Poland pages.

Mandatory value:
- likely pickup points,
- local transport logic,
- practical contact expectations.

### 4. Route Pages
Purpose:
- capture the strongest high-intent searches,
- answer the exact operational need.

Mandatory value:
- route-specific description,
- pickup from hospital / mortuary / funeral home,
- formalities,
- road vs air,
- preparation for transport,
- delivery to Poland,
- family support,
- 24/7 contact,
- route FAQ.

### 5. Procedural FAQ Pages
Purpose:
- absorb informational intent that is close to conversion,
- reduce stress and uncertainty,
- pass users into service and route pages.

Mandatory value:
- direct answer,
- step-by-step practical guidance,
- risks / common mistakes,
- urgent CTA.

## Page Relationships

- International hub -> main service pages
- Main service pages -> country pages
- Country pages -> city pages
- City pages -> route pages
- Route pages -> formalities / air transport / contact / related routes
- Procedural FAQ pages -> service page + relevant country page + relevant route page

This relationship map should be treated as mandatory internal linking logic, not optional enrichment.

## Reusable Section Inventory

### Hero urgent service block
Inputs:
- service or route name,
- geography,
- urgency signal,
- trust claim,
- main CTA.

Uniqueness comes from:
- route specificity,
- local logistics,
- transport mode emphasis.

### Pickup sources
Inputs:
- hospital,
- mortuary,
- funeral home,
- airport handover when relevant.

Uniqueness comes from:
- local institution patterns,
- urban vs regional access,
- airport role.

### Documents and formalities
Inputs:
- country documentation,
- release documents,
- family authorisation,
- consular elements,
- translations.

Uniqueness comes from:
- country differences,
- road vs air differences,
- body vs urn differences.

### Road vs air transport
Inputs:
- route length,
- airport access,
- urgency,
- cost logic,
- legal constraints.

Uniqueness comes from:
- actual route profile,
- nearby airports,
- realistic road corridor.

### Timing and organisation
Inputs:
- release dependencies,
- route length,
- mode of transport,
- weekend/public holiday constraints.

Uniqueness comes from:
- city distance,
- airport dependence,
- local paperwork speed.

### Family support
Inputs:
- what the company takes over,
- language support,
- how family communication works,
- no-upfront-payment explanation.

Uniqueness comes from:
- route pressure,
- country complexity,
- whether family is abroad or already in Poland.

### Trust proof
Inputs:
- family business,
- 30+ years,
- competitive pricing,
- no upfront payment,
- 5-star reputation,
- own vehicle,
- 24/7 speed.

Uniqueness comes from:
- selecting the most relevant trust proof for the page intent.

## Content Brief Rules

Every page brief must define:
- page type,
- primary intent,
- primary keyword,
- supporting keywords,
- route / city / country context,
- required sections,
- mandatory trust elements,
- CTA goal,
- internal links,
- FAQ questions,
- manual notes.

Every page must avoid:
- swapping only the city name,
- repeating generic filler,
- fake “local history” or travel fluff,
- over-optimized keyword repetition,
- machine-translated sounding phrasing.

Every page must deliver:
- practical next-step clarity,
- emotional calm,
- strong trust,
- easy contact path,
- route-relevant operational details.
