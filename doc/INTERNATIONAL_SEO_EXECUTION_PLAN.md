# International SEO Execution Plan

This document is the execution plan for building international organic visibility for funeral repatriation and transport of remains to Poland.

## Goal

Rank for high-intent queries such as:

- `transport zwlok [miasto] Polska`
- `sprowadzenie ciala z [kraj] do Polski`
- `repatriation of remains to Poland`
- `body transport [city] to Poland`
- `Uberfuhrung [city] Polen`
- `rapatriement du corps [ville] Pologne`

The target is not generic traffic. The target is urgent, conversion-oriented search from families and decision-makers who need to move a deceased person to Poland.

## Core SEO Model

The architecture should work as:

- `homepage` -> trust, brand, main service links
- `international service hub` -> main commercial cluster
- `country pages` -> country-specific logistics and documents
- `route pages` -> highest-intent pages
- `procedural FAQ pages` -> informational support that feeds service and route pages
- `contact 24/7` -> universal conversion endpoint

## On-Page SEO Checklist

For every page:

- unique title
- unique meta description
- clear single H1
- route- or country-specific lead
- canonical
- hreflang
- visible phone CTA above the fold
- internal links to next-step pages
- FAQ only if it is real and visible
- structured data only where supported by visible content

For route pages specifically:

- pickup source types
- road vs air explanation
- document reality
- delivery into Poland
- family support
- urgent contact block

## Indexing Checklist

- page has unique value
- page is linked internally
- page is present in sitemap
- self-canonical is correct
- hreflang set is complete
- no placeholder or near-duplicate text
- no accidental `noindex`
- priority pages requested in Search Console

## Sitemap Submission Checklist

- submit canonical sitemap to Search Console
- ensure only indexable URLs are included
- refresh after each rollout batch
- validate alternates and locale URLs
- segment by type later if route count grows significantly

## Search Console Setup

Recommended setup:

  - `/pl/`
  - `/de/`

- route-page performance view
- country-page performance view

Watch closely:

- duplicate without user-selected canonical
- alternate with proper canonical
- soft 404
- indexed but low-quality clusters

## Content Expansion Logic Without Spam

Expand only when at least one is true:

- the route has meaningful search demand
- the route has different logistics
- the route has different paperwork reality
- the route supports a stronger parent country cluster

Do not publish:

- city pages with only name swaps
- multiple pages with the same intent and no added value
- low-value route combinations before priority routes perform

## How To Avoid Doorway / Thin Content Issues

- do not substitute only the city name
- make route pages different in logistics, documents, transport mode and FAQ
- merge weak pages into stronger country pages if they cannot justify indexation
- keep city pages navigational, not doorway-like
- only scale where the cluster is already internally linked and strategically justified

## Authority Building

Build authority with:

- consistent legal identity and NAP
- 30+ years and family-business trust signals
- clear no-upfront-payment explanation
- route pages supported by country, formalities and FAQ clusters
- manually reviewed DE/EN/FR copy on top pages
- practical, procedural clarity instead of generic marketing

## 30 / 60 / 90 Day Roadmap

### 60 days

### 90 days

- expand to second-wave countries
- improve CTR on pages with impressions but weak clicks
- prune or merge low-value pages
- keep route expansion quality-first

## Risks

- thin route pages
- route overexpansion before top pages perform
- weak localization quality in DE/FR/EN
- duplicate intent between route and country pages
- incomplete canonical or hreflang implementation
- orphaned route pages
- procedural pages with no conversion path
- too many weak pages consuming crawl budget
